Advent of code : A Month Late,
Still Determined

"The best time to start Advent of Code was December 12th.
The second-best time is now."

I discovered Advent of Code in a slightly unconventional way , through a company application process. By the time I found it, December had already come and gone. Coincidentally, it was January 12th.

Starting that late could’ve been an excuse not to start at all. But I decided to commit anyway. The goal was never speed or rankings , it was consistency and learning in public.

In December, I had an advent calendar from Maltesers one sweet treat to open each day. So I started thinking about this challenge the same way. Think of it like an advent calendar. December had one made of chocolate. January got one made of puzzles. Both are sweet , one just happens to be better for my brain.

And honestly, holding onto a bit of the Christmas vibe during cold winter days isn’t such a bad thing, right? 🎄

Learnings & Reflections

// Consistency wins

Motivation is a feeling it comes and goes. Consistency is a system. Showing up daily, even for just 30 minutes, made the puzzles feel less like challenges and more like a ritual I genuinely looked forward to. By day four, it wasn’t about motivation anymore it was simply part of my day.

// AI is a thinking partner, not a shortcut

I used AI throughout this challenge , for hints when stuck, for explanations when I didn't understand why my approach wasn't working. What surprised me was how much I actually learned from those interactions. Used well, AI accelerates understanding, it doesn't shortcut the process , ir strengthens it.

// Small wins compound

22 stars out of 23. Not perfect. But each one felt earned, each one built on the last. By the end I wasn't the same programmer I was on day one not Not in some heroic obernight transformation...but in a quietly meaningful way. Progress over perfection is the whole point.

// The public part matters

Documenting this publicly changed how I approached each day. Knowing I'd commit it, share it, write about it , that added a layer of intentionality. I wasn't just solving puzzles in a vacuum. I was building a record of showing up.

If you're reading this and thinking about starting late too: start anyway.
